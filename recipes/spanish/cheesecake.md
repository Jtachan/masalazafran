# Spanish cheesecake (Cuajada & queso)

The traditional version of the spanish cheesecake (not to confuse with the basque cheesecake) is done without an oven.
This is one of those recipes that you must be patient and follow the instructions.

---

**Ingredients**

- _Butter_ (100 g)
- _Tea biscuits_ — Use "Maria biscuits" if you have them available.
- _Cream cheese_ (125 g)
- _Milk_ (1 L)
- _Sugar_ (200 g)
- _Egg yolks_ (3)
- _Gelatin sheets_ (2) — This is usually "cuajada en polvo" if you are in Spain.

---

**Steps**

1. With a food processor make crumbles from all the biscuits.
2. Mix the crumbles with the butter and then put it in a cake-mold, compressing it. You can use the bottom of a glass to compress it.
3. Disolve the gelatin in 200 mL of warm milk.
4. In the same milk mix well the egg yolks and the cream cheese.
5. Heat up the rest of the milk. Whenever it is going to start boiling, lower the heat to medium-low.
6. Add the suggar and the other 200 mL of milk with the other ingredients. 
7. Pour the mix over the cookie-base made before. Let it chill for :clock: 30~40 minutes at room temperature, then cool it in the fridge for at least :clock: 4 hours.
